The first step in undersatanding how to position elements is to understand the fundamental model of CSS-the box model.

The Box Model

Every element you place on a page creates a box. You cannot always see the boundries and the borders of the boxes are there. A div element creates a box, as do h1,img,span,and td elements.Each box has propertiesyou can set with CSS. I've illustrated some of these properties in Figure 1.

Figure 1 Border,Padding,Margin

The padding of box is the distnce between the content of the box and the box's border,and the margin is the distance between a box and any adjacent element.In other words, padding adds space inside the border, while a margin adds space outside the border.In the following CSS, I set both the Padding and the margin to a distance of 10px.
